Foreward

Lately when I wake up and look at the Africa around me I recognize the opportunity this new Africa represents in the age of AI and smart technology.The possibilities that abound with the use of data and solutions that can change lives in Health, Education, Deep-Tech, Research, Fintech, Agritech and Climate Change.

Africa must embrace and adopt AI as a key, if not the most important, form of technology to make sure we are truly part of the 4th Industrial Revolution.At the moment we are not doing a very good job in AI across the continent and falling behind in four key areas, namely;

1. Investing in AI research across the continent
2. Training more AI engineers & creating an enabling environment for them to become creative.
3. Investing in AI start-ups in Africa.
4. Investing in Infrastructure required to support AI.

I believe a data driven ecosystem with innovation at the center of it, and education as a core value, is critical if we are going to improve the state of AI in Africa. We must educate everyone on the value chain to become better experienced and knowledge about the value, as well as the urgent need, for an increased focus on AI in the continent.

Currently a number of factors are frustrating the growth of the AI ecosystem allied to the lack of African business leaders who are knowledgeable enough to advocate for AI in the continent.I believe the opportunity lies in the start-up space to become a key driver for the






development of the AI ecosystem in Africa.
As a continent we have abundant human talent and resources. If applied accurately AI can help tackle a number of social and economic problems across the continent.

Some key African countries have started driving the conversation, namely; Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, Rwanda, Ghana, Egypt, Tunisia and Senegal.

Connecting the important actors and experts within the field of AI is also of immense importance, with communities such as Zindi, AICE, Data Science Nigeria to name but a few building AI labs and education networks of young professionals.

I believe that Africa is in a fantastic position to drive and embrace AI. Free flow of data across various sectors as well as the big companies coming to Africa must support the growth of the start-ups in AI and also help governments understand the value of investment in AI.

Finally, we are now slowly seeing the funding of African AI start-ups but we need more early stage AI companies in Africa getting funded for the space to grow rapidly.
